Harbourvale City MRT - a history

Much has been said about the history of this City. In fact, the history for the conceptualization of Harbourvale City Rapid Transit spans back to 1982 (in Singapore retrospective)!

1982 - The beginning of a whole new rail system

In 12 Aug 1965, after Harbourvale gained independence after Singapore gained it on 9 Aug. The Goverment faced problems with road congestions. So in 1973 they Introduced Buses. but it can't solve in 1990s, So they decided to go on on a rail System.

Harbourvale Line 1987

HCRT was founded to Operate Harbourvale Line. The Harbourvale Line operated in the middle center side of Harbourvale City The 1st Phase of Harbourvale Line is between B3 Harbourvale Highway & H8 Bayshore, The 2nd Phase was between B4/W1 North Coast and B8 Blackton Junction, The Final Phase was from H7 Kegstone to H1 Harbourvale. H2 Was Wynyard and was closed as contracters of other building in the area was blocking off space to make way for the MRT Station until 1988 to finally start Construction Decorations, Wynyard was constructed with the other stations but some workers came in for development of the Wynyard area & the .

North West Line

North West Line was opened befor Stage 2 of Harbourvale Line. Which ran for a short 4 Stations W1 North Coast - W4 Lakeview Commuters could see a block tunnel with tracks which was Stage 2 of the line E5 District to N7 Blacton South E as East from center of NWL.

Harbourvale Airport Express Line (now Airport Express Line)

After a little debate of HCRT, they decided to construct Airport Express for a Branch of Harbourvale Line. But in 1989 They Decided to cancel the branch and make it a single Line so it was named as Airport Express Line.

Kawasaki Heavy Industries C100A

For the Rail System they contracted Kawasaki and all of the Company of Kawasaki who was working on the Real KHI in 1985 and contracted Contract 100A. All Motors was the same and the units delivered was the same as the real KHI C151

Blackton Line

As the eastern and western parts of the city grew, the government announced the Blackton Line to be built and operated by HCRT But Blackton Line was abandoned.

Evergreen Fields Line

Although HCRT was supposed to begin operations on this line by 2008, the line was abandoned due to flaws in signalling and the land that the line was built on sank tremendously. HLRT (Harbourvale Light Rail Transport) bidded for the new line and won the bid originally and renamed as Evergreen Fields LRT, but due to financial problems (in reality, it's lack of time.), the line was given up to HCRT and HLRT was ceased.

Coastal Sands Line

As there was a "blank spot" between City Line and Airport Express Line, the Coastal Sands Line was supposed to help passengers get from Coney Island (Yes, you heard it. It's called Coney Island) to East Harbour quicker. HMRT was awarded the line's tender but ceased and given to HCRT and HMRT was ceased by HCRT.

Kegstone Line

There was not much a need to build this line, until Caroye began tremendous developement. HCRT was awarded this line's tender. Construction has started. WTC is the official contractor of the line. But The Line was no longer in use and got abandoned.

City Line

Much like the Circle Line in Singapore, this line is basically... a circle. However, due to underdevelopement in several areas of the coastal areas, this line was cancelled and abandoned.

Second System Map Refresh

As the design of the old map began to look dated, a tender was put up to redesign the system map. TDC (Transport Design Consultancy) eventually won the tender. This also marked the first time HCRT did a reorganization of operators, and halted several lines due to rising costs. These include the City Line, and the cancelled Western Harbourvale Extension.

The LRT systems like the District LRT and Harbourvale City Light Rail (actually all LRT lines in Harbourvale) were privatized, hence they were not included in the official system map.

2020 - Acquisition by SMRT Virtual (now SMRTO)

2020 - 2023

In 2020, SMRT Virtual (now SMRTO) took over operations of the HCRT. The KHI C100A were decommissioned, replaced by our exisitng C651/A/B/C/D. Announcements were also replaced, and signages refreshed.

Third System Map Refresh

In tandem with Singapore's move to the new Wayfinding System, we refreshed our system map with new map and caplet designs.

Fourth System Map Refresh

Several iterations of the new Wayfinding Systems were made, hence, we decided to refresh the maps yet again, in 2023 and 2024.

2024 - Major Upgrading, Replacement Works

As HCRT was ceased by SMV SkyPeak Transportations re-founded HCRT as a remix company and the C651A/B/C/D was gone sent to Singapore for CLD,CDD & STARiS. But the MRT did not went so well as SMRTO Contracted 9 Years for Exsisting HCRT Lines. The C651A-D in SMRTO's Fleet was scrapped but SkyPeak Went to collect all the scrap materials and remaining trains from the C651A-D and the materials were sent back to Kawasaki & Alstom the manufactures who made SMRTO's 2020-4 fleet. The HCRT was then called back to Bukit Batok, Singapore as SkyPeak is a HDB in Bukit Batok. when the C651A-D were re-completed the HCRT was gone back to Harbourvale to do repairs in-house there. CDD STARiS VFD and CLD repaired The Line was resumed where it came back from. In 2024 HCRT Contracted Kawasaki for C100A again for the Resumation of HCRT, 32 units was delivered. the HCRT also started Phase 3 of NWL which completed the NWL with NW8 Caryone - NW10 City Square
